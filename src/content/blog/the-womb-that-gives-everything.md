The placenta is an organ that develops in the uterus during pregnancy and acts as the medium between the mother and the baby, exchanging nutrients and oxygen from the mother to the foetus while carrying waste substances from the foetus back to the mother. The placenta helps in excretion, respiration, provides nutrients, and even acts as a shock absorber for the baby. The baby cannot survive independently inside the mother without its help; hence, it has been controversial and highly debated for decades whether a foetus can be considered a parasite on the mother.

![the womb that gives everything](/uploads/thewombthatgiveseverything.jpg)

A woman struggles through three full trimesters—that's around 280 days of nausea, vomiting, exhaustion, hormonal fluctuations, backpain, bloating, swollen feet, sleepless nights, mood swings, food cravings, physical discomfort, labour pain, sweat, extreme weight gain, and the inability to rely on painkillers or any major drugs. She carries a body that is deformed in every biological sense, yet beautiful and adored all the same. After overcoming all of this—along with the societal expectations and mental anxiety of producing a child, or worse, a boy for a conservative Indian family to continue the lineage (*vansh*)—she finally becomes a **MOTHER**. By then, she has spent nine whole months feeding her child with her own lifeforce, almost as if she were a host supplying nutrients to a parasite that thrives in the womb while the mother suffers and society gets excited for the baby. Just to get thrashed by the same child when they grow up in this fucked-up society, who may not even care for her, but might instead leave her in an old-age home or abandon her simply to live their own life, while still feeding like a parasite on the mother's strength, hope, love, sacrifice, patience, and unconditional care. The point is that even after birth, the child continues to thrive by feeding from the same mother—not through the placenta anymore, but through her time, energy, sleep, emotions, dreams, money, and the countless pieces of herself that she willingly gives away without ever expecting them back.

The female human body is a fascinating, magical wonder that creates life. A woman possesses the mental strength, emotional resilience, and endurance to withstand pain, sacrifice, heartbreak, and often even male stupidity, yet still finds the capacity to love, trust, nurture, forgive, and hope again. That is perhaps the greatest biological miracle of all. Maybe that's why calling motherhood *"natural"* has always felt like an understatement—because there is nothing ordinary about creating another human being while slowly giving away pieces of yourself in the process. If a parasite is something that survives by depending entirely on its host, then motherhood may be the only relationship where the host willingly gives everything—and calls it love.
