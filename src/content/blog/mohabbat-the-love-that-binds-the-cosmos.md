> **"जब मिले थोड़ी फुरसत,
> खुद से कर ले मोहब्बत।
> है तुझे भी इजाज़त,
> कर ले तू भी मोहब्बत।"**


Is it something you simply feel?

Is it something you can't explain to anyone?

Is it something that everyone needs and experiences?

Or is it sitting quietly in a corner, gazing at the most beautiful person in the world, knowing they can never be yours?

Or is it thinking about them all the time, hoping they might think about you too?

Is it waiting for the blue tick, expecting a reply?

Or is it caring for them, no matter what?

Is it hating them one moment, yet skipping a heartbeat the instant you see them the next?

Is love black or white?

I think it's neither; it is simply grey. It can never truly be explained in words, yet it is reflected throughout the cosmos. Love brings new life. Perhaps love gave birth to us after the Big Bang. Perhaps there is no dark matter or dark energy. Perhaps it is simply love that binds and governs the vast universe.

![mohabbat](/uploads/mohabbat.jpg)

**"Mohabbat"** is an Urdu word that comes from Persian. It describes a form of love so deep that you never want to be separated from the people you cherish. It is loving someone with all your heart, doing everything you can to stay by their side. Yet, if their happiness lies elsewhere, you are willing to let them go, because their happiness matters more than your own.
Mohabbat is not limited to romance; it belongs to everyone—family, relatives, friends, and someone special.
Love does not discriminate.
It doesn't care whether you are short or tall, fair or dark, fat or skinny, young or old, rich or poor. It doesn't care whether you are Muslim or Hindu.
Sometimes, love is nothing more than an unspoken truth.
A truth you may fear to express, yet carry deep within your heart, cherishing every moment it gives you.
Is love simply posting selfies with your partner?
Or is it looking into their eyes when they are close to you, wishing you could simply hold them and kiss them?
Is love merely dumb luck?
Or does everyone have someone meant just for them?

We know the stories of profound and timeless love:
* Romeo and Juliet
* Laila and Majnun
* Siya and Ram
* Radha and Krishna
* Cleopatra and Mark Antony
* Rose and Jack

There are two things common to almost every great love story in the world:

**Love and misery.**

But why?
Why does misery seem to accompany true love?
Why can't love always be simple, peaceful, and fulfilling?
Then, is love nothing more than confusion and anxiety?
Or is it the overwhelming sense of euphoria you feel when you finally see that one person after a long time?
People say love is eternal.
But how?
We know that matter and energy can neither be created nor destroyed; they only transform from one form into another.
Perhaps love is the same.
So the next time you find yourself sitting alone in a corner, believing it's over—that you've lost love, that you've lost everything—remember this:

**Love is cosmic.**

And the cosmos can never truly be lost, because it is what binds us together and shapes who we are.
We must first discover the love—the light—that lies hidden deep within ourselves. Only then can we share it with the world.
And perhaps, only then, can we begin to understand the true meaning of love.
